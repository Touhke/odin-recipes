Recette Du Koki Made In Cameroun

Home Page

Description

Koki is a cherished traditional dish from Cameroon made primarily from black‑eyed peas (or cowpeas), which are soaked, peeled, ground into a paste, then enriched with palm oil and seasonings, wrapped in banana leaves (or foil) and steamed until firm. It is often prepared for family gatherings and festive occasions, representing culinary ingenuity in using locally available legumes, palm oil, and leaves for cooking.

Ingredients

  • ~500 g black‑eyed peas (cowpeas), soaked overnight.
  • ~100 g kale or spinach (optional) or another green leafy.
  • ~40‑60 ml palm oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped.
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced.
  • 1 Scotch bonnet or habanero pepper (optional, for heat).
  • Salt, bouillon cube or seasoning cube to taste.
  • Banana leaves or aluminum foil for wrapping.

Steps

  1. Soak the black‑eyed peas overnight until they have doubled in size.
  2. Remove skins/peel the soaked beans, then blend or grind them into a thick paste.
  3. In a bowl, mix the bean paste with chopped onion, minced garlic, pepper (if using), greens (if using), palm oil, salt and seasoning.
  4. Prepare banana leaves: wash and soften them (e.g., blanch quickly) then lay them out.
  5. Steam the wrapped parcels over simmering water for about 45‑60 minutes (or until set) depending on size.
  6. Remove from steam, unwrap carefully and serve warm. Traditionally served with plantains, cassava or bread.
